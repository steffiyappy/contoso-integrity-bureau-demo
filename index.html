Exercise 1 · Daily BriefingResearcher · Critique

How the Task Force Head starts the day in 2 minutes. Open Microsoft 365 Copilot at copilot.microsoft.com → click Researcher. Keep the model picker on Auto (this is Critique - dual-model with self-review). Paste the prompt below.

Researcher reads your inbox, calendar, Teams chats, and recent files - then hands the Task Force Head a single, structured briefing for the day. No more spending the first 30 minutes catching up.

Task 1.1🔍 Researcher · Auto (Critique)

Generate today's Task Force daily briefing

Create a daily briefing from emails, messages, documents, and meetings to help me prepare for the current workday. Use your understanding of the projects I work on, workstreams I care about, and key people I work with to understand what is relevant and important. Sections: A short intro paragraph that provides an overview of my day, i.e. what a personal assistant would tell me to greet me in the morning. Key updates since yesterday - Include informational updates from the last workday that are likely to impact my work and collaborations. Suggested tasks for today - Tasks or questions assigned to me that I should handle today. Cross-check that suggested tasks haven't been completed already. List unread email threads from the past week that likely need my reply-prioritize those where I'm on To/CC, @mentioned, or involve requests, deadlines, leadership, or customers. Exclude automated messages. Show only urgent or important items in a concise table, and draft replies where needed. If none require action, say: 'I didn't find any unread threads that require a reply.' Meeting prep for today - Upcoming meetings only, shown in a table with: Meeting (time, title, organizer), Topic Overview, and Suggested Preparations. Note if inferred or not needed. Rules: Start with a header: Daily briefing - Day, Date. Follow with a sub-header stating the timestamp of generation. Start each section with a meaningful emoji. Use only explicitly stated facts; no speculation, interpretations or predictions. If a section has no data, state that.
What success looks like: within 90 seconds you have a one-screen brief: overview paragraph, key updates since yesterday, today's tasks, unread threads that need a reply (with drafts), and a meeting-prep table - all grounded on real signals from your tenant, with citations.
Bonus🔍 Researcher · follow-up

Drill in on the single most important item

Pick one item from the briefing that needs deeper thinking. Ask Researcher:

Take the highest-priority item from the briefing above. Pull together everything I've received on it in the last 14 days - emails, chats, files, meeting notes. Summarise the current state in 5 bullets, list the open decisions, and draft one short message I should send today to move it forward.

Exercise 2 · OSINT News ExtractionResearcher · Copilot Chat with URL grounding · Flagship for Enforcement

Why this matters. Many corruption cases are not reported through the official SPDP Online system. The Task Force must sweep open-source news, extract the facts, and add rows to the case register. Copilot Chat, grounded on a URL, does the extraction in seconds; Researcher Critique does the audit pass.
Task 2.1💬 Copilot Chat · one URL to one row

Turn a news article into a structured SPDP row

Open Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat. Paste the prompt below, which includes a live sample URL from Antara News about the Jember bansos case. Copilot Chat will fetch and read the article, then return a single table row you can paste into the case register.

Goal: extract the corruption case facts from the news article at the URL below and return them as a single row I can paste into my SPDP case register. Context: I am the Head of the Coordination and Supervision Task Force at Contoso Integrity Bureau. I sweep open-source Indonesian news for corruption cases that may not yet be in SPDP Online, and I need clean, structured data to add to the register. Source URL: https://jatim.antaranews.com/berita/261998/saksi-bpkp-sebut-kerugian-negara-korupsi-hibah-bansos-jember-capai-rp104-miliar Expectation: return a Markdown table with exactly one data row and the following columns, in this order: 1. Case Name (short descriptive title, English) 2. Case Period (year or year range as stated in the article) 3. Suspect(s) or Defendant(s) (comma-separated names as reported) 4. Estimated State Financial Loss in IDR (numeric, plus a human-readable form like "104 billion IDR") 5. Law Enforcement Agency (KPK, Kejaksaan, Polri, or as reported) 6. Current Case Stage (investigation, indictment, trial, verdict, appeal, or as reported) 7. Source URL (repeat the URL above) 8. Extraction Confidence (High, Medium, or Low, based on how clearly the article states each field) 9. Notes (one short line for anything ambiguous, e.g., "state loss figure attributed to BPKP witness, not final court ruling") Rules: only use facts explicitly stated in the article. If a field is not stated, write "not stated" rather than guessing. Do not add fields that were not requested.
What success looks like: a clean one-row table with each field grounded on the article. Confidence should be High for state loss and agency, Medium or Low for stage if the article does not clearly say.
Task 2.2💬 Copilot Chat · sweep five URLs

Batch extract five articles into one consolidated table

Paste up to five URLs at once (use the ones in 04 Contoso_Sample_News_URLs.md) and ask for a single consolidated table with a de-duplication rule.

Goal: sweep the five news articles at the URLs below and return one consolidated table of corruption cases for my SPDP case register. Context: I run a daily OSINT sweep across Indonesian news. Multiple articles sometimes cover the same case (same suspect, same case period), and I do not want duplicate rows in my register. Source URLs: 1. https://jatim.antaranews.com/berita/261998/saksi-bpkp-sebut-kerugian-negara-korupsi-hibah-bansos-jember-capai-rp104-miliar 2. [paste URL 2 from 04 Contoso_Sample_News_URLs.md] 3. [paste URL 3] 4. [paste URL 4] 5. [paste URL 5] Expectation: - One consolidated Markdown table with the same 9 columns as before (Case Name, Case Period, Suspects, State Loss IDR, Agency, Stage, Source URL, Confidence, Notes). - One row per unique case. De-duplication rule: if two articles refer to the same case (same suspect names AND overlapping case period), merge them into a single row, list the most complete State Loss figure, and put both source URLs in the Source URL cell separated by a semicolon. - Sort by Estimated State Loss descending. - After the table, add a short "Sweep summary" paragraph: total articles read, total unique cases, total estimated state loss, and any article that failed to load or was paywalled. Rules: only facts explicitly stated in the articles. Flag conflicts between articles (for example, different suspect names for the same case) in the Notes column.
Task 2.3🔍 Researcher · Critique · auditor pass

Verify the extraction against the source text

Switch to Researcher and set the model picker to Auto (Critique). This is the auditor pass: Researcher re-reads the same article, checks each field the extractor produced, and flags any hallucination risk.

Goal: audit the extraction table I produced from the news article and flag any field that is not clearly supported by the source text. Context: I am the Head of the Coordination and Supervision Task Force. Before a case row is entered into our official SPDP register, a second reviewer must verify every field against the article. You are that second reviewer. Source URL: https://jatim.antaranews.com/berita/261998/saksi-bpkp-sebut-kerugian-negara-korupsi-hibah-bansos-jember-capai-rp104-miliar Extraction to audit: [paste the one-row table you produced in Task 2.1] Expectation: return a verification table with one row per field, columns: - Field Name - Extracted Value - Source Evidence (quote the sentence or phrase in the article that supports the value, in the original language) - Verdict (Supported, Partially Supported, Unsupported, or Not in Source) - Hallucination Risk (High, Medium, Low) - Recommended Fix (leave blank if Supported) After the table, write one paragraph with your overall confidence in the extraction and whether you would recommend the row is entered into SPDP as-is, entered with corrections, or rejected pending manual review. Rules: if the article does not clearly support a field, say so. Do not fill in gaps with external knowledge.
Reality check: extraction quality depends on article completeness. Some outlets bury the state-loss figure or attribute it to a witness rather than a court finding. Always cross-check the state-loss number against the actual source paragraph before entering the row into the SPDP register. Treat any row with Medium or Low confidence as "pending manual review".

Exercise 3 · Procurement Anomaly DetectionCopilot in Excel · Edit · Flagship for Prevention

Why this matters. Provincial and regency governments submit Excel procurement disclosures every quarter. Buried in 180 rows of a Q3 file are the exact patterns the Task Force cares about: contracts split to stay under the direct-procurement threshold, one vendor dominating an agency, price outliers, brand-new vendors winning large awards, shared vendor addresses, and awards signed on Sundays. This is where Copilot earns its keep.

Open 02 Contoso_Direct_Procurement_Q3.xlsx. Click the Copilot icon in the ribbon. Set the mode to Edit. The file has these columns: Contract ID, Procurement Year, Agency, Procurement Package Name, Vendor Name, Vendor Address, Vendor NPWP, Contract Value IDR, Award Date, Procurement Method, Contract Start, Contract End, Notes.

Task 3.1📊 Copilot in Excel · Edit

Build the anomaly detection layer and Governance Dashboard in one prompt

Goal: transform this direct-procurement disclosure into a governance scorecard. Add anomaly signals, compute a risk score, apply conditional formatting, build a pivot summary, and create a Governance Dashboard sheet. Context: I am the Head of the Coordination and Supervision Task Force at Contoso Integrity Bureau. The file contains 180 direct-procurement contracts from provinces and regencies for Q3. Under Indonesian rules, the direct-procurement threshold is 200 million IDR. Contracts split just under that threshold, especially by the same vendor within a short window, are a classic red flag. Source columns available in the Contracts sheet: Contract ID, Procurement Year, Agency, Procurement Package Name, Vendor Name, Vendor Address, Vendor NPWP, Contract Value IDR, Award Date, Procurement Method, Contract Start, Contract End, Notes. Expectation: 1. Add six computed columns to the right of the Contracts table, one per anomaly signal, each returning 1 or 0: a. Split_Contract_Flag: 1 if the same Vendor Name + same Agency has 3 or more contracts each under 200,000,000 IDR within any rolling 30-day window based on Award Date, else 0. b. Vendor_Concentration_Flag: 1 if the vendor's total value at that agency for Q3 exceeds 60 percent of the agency's total Q3 spend, else 0. c. Price_Outlier_Flag: 1 if the Contract Value for a given Procurement Package Name is more than 2 times the median value for the same package name across all agencies, else 0. d. New_Vendor_High_Value_Flag: 1 if the vendor's NPWP was registered less than 6 months before the Award Date AND the Contract Value exceeds 1,000,000,000 IDR, else 0. (Assume NPWP age is available in the Notes field, e.g., "NPWP registered 2025-03-15"; parse it there.) e. Shared_Address_Flag: 1 if the exact Vendor Address is shared by two or more different Vendor Names anywhere in the file, else 0. f. Weekend_Award_Flag: 1 if the Award Date falls on a Saturday or Sunday, else 0. 2. Add a Risk_Score column equal to the sum of the six flag columns (0 to 6). Add a Risk_Tier column: 0 = Clean, 1-2 = Watch, 3-4 = Elevated, 5-6 = Critical. 3. Apply conditional formatting to Risk_Score: green for 0, yellow for 1-2, orange for 3-4, red for 5-6. 4. Create a Pivot Table on a new sheet named "Pivot" with Agency in rows, Risk_Tier in columns, and Count of Contract ID as values. 5. Create a new sheet named "Governance Dashboard" with: - A title cell "Q3 Procurement Governance Scorecard - Contoso Integrity Bureau" - Six scorecard boxes, one per anomaly signal, each showing the count of contracts flagged and the total IDR value of those contracts. - A callout box listing the Top 10 highest-risk contracts by Contract ID, Agency, Vendor Name, Contract Value IDR, and Risk_Score, sorted by Risk_Score descending then Contract Value descending. - A short "Where to look first" text box with the single agency and single vendor that carry the highest combined risk. Rules: use existing columns only. Do not delete rows. Keep the original Contracts sheet intact; put all new views on new sheets. If a field cannot be parsed (for example, NPWP age not in Notes), leave the flag as 0 and add a comment cell.
What success looks like: the Contracts sheet gains eight new columns (six flags plus Risk_Score plus Risk_Tier) with conditional formatting. The Pivot sheet shows which agencies cluster in Elevated and Critical. The Governance Dashboard reads like an audit exhibit, with the top 10 contracts named.
Task 3.2📊 Copilot in Excel · follow-up · single-agency brief

Zoom into one agency and produce a mini brief

Goal: filter the Contracts sheet to a single agency and produce a one-page mini brief on that agency's anomaly pattern that I can attach to a supervision letter. Context: pick the agency with the highest count of Critical-tier contracts from the Pivot. If two agencies are tied, pick the one with the higher total Contract Value across all its contracts. Expectation: create a new sheet named "Agency Brief" with: - Agency name, total Q3 contracts, total IDR value, count of flagged contracts, and Risk_Score distribution as a small bar of counts. - The top three vendors at this agency by total IDR value, with their concentration percentage. - A table of every Critical-tier contract at this agency, with Contract ID, Package Name, Vendor Name, Contract Value IDR, Award Date, and which flags were tripped (as a comma-separated list of flag names). - Three bullet observations in plain English suitable for a Task Force supervision letter. Do not name individuals; refer to agencies and vendor names only. Rules: use only the data in the workbook. If any observation depends on external context, mark it clearly with "verification required".
Reality check: interactive slicers and pivot filters sometimes need to be reapplied after a Copilot Edit run. Before you rely on the Risk_Score for a supervision decision, verify that the six flag formulas match the Bureau's official Q3 scoring rubric, and spot-check three Critical-tier rows against the raw disclosure. Copilot builds the exhibit fast; the auditor still signs it.

Exercise 4 · Meeting MinutesTeams Copilot recap · Word Copilot polish · not Cowork

The scenario. Pak Bambang just stepped out of the weekly Task Force coordination meeting. Six people, 30 minutes, covering SPDP backlog, three red-flag agencies from this week's procurement sweep, and one urgent OSINT case from Jember. The meeting was recorded with transcript on. This is the fastest path from meeting to house-style minutes.
Task 4.1👥 Teams Copilot · post-meeting recap

Ask Teams Copilot for a structured recap

In the Teams meeting chat, open Copilot from the recap panel. Paste the prompt below.

Goal: give me a structured recap of the Task Force coordination meeting that just ended. Context: I chaired the meeting. The Bureau is looking for clear action items, decisions, risks, and per-attendee commitments so nothing slips through the week. Expectation: 1. One-paragraph overall summary (three sentences maximum). 2. Decisions table: Decision, Who owns follow-through, Confirmed by. 3. Action items table: Action, Owner, Due date, Status (Open). 4. Risks and blockers: bullet list, each with a short mitigation suggestion. 5. Per-attendee commitments: one bullet per named attendee summarising what they personally committed to. 6. Next steps and next meeting agenda: three to five bullets. Rules: only cite things actually said in the meeting. If an owner or due date was not stated, mark it "to be confirmed". Do not invent risks that were not raised.
Task 4.2📝 Word Copilot · formal minutes from transcript

Draft formal Bureau-house-style minutes

Open a blank Word document. Click the Copilot icon, then Draft with Copilot. Reference the transcript file 06 Contoso_Task_Force_Meeting_Transcript.docx.

Goal: draft formal meeting minutes for the Task Force weekly coordination meeting in the Bureau's house style. Context: I chair this meeting. Attendees include the Enforcement Lead, Prevention Lead, Data Analyst, Legal Advisor, and OSINT Specialist. Minutes go to the Bureau Secretariat, the Deputy for Prevention, and the Deputy for Enforcement. Source: use the attached transcript file 06 Contoso_Task_Force_Meeting_Transcript.docx as the sole factual basis. Expectation: produce a Word document with these numbered sections in order: 1. Header block: meeting title, date, time, location, chair, minute taker, attendees, apologies. 2. Agenda summary (three to five bullets, phrased as topics not verbs). 3. Discussion notes: one subsection per agenda item, three to six sentences each, past tense, neutral. 4. Decisions box: a bordered call-out listing each decision on its own numbered line. 5. Action register: a table with columns Action, Owner, Due Date, Priority (High, Medium, Low), Status. 6. Risks and escalations: bullet list. 7. Distribution list: Bureau Secretariat, Deputy for Prevention, Deputy for Enforcement, all attendees. Rules: use only content from the transcript. Neutral tone. No opinions. Do not attribute a decision to a person who did not verbally agree to it in the transcript.
Task 4.3📝 Word Copilot · polish · two-page ceiling

Tighten to two pages with an executive summary

Goal: polish the draft minutes for clarity and length, then add an executive summary paragraph at the top. Context: the Deputy for Enforcement reads only the first page of any minutes. If the essentials are not on page one, the escalation is missed. Expectation: - Improve sentence clarity, reduce jargon, and shorten wordy phrases without changing meaning. - Enforce a two-page ceiling. If the draft is longer, trim the discussion notes first (never the decisions box or the action register). - Add a three-sentence Executive Summary at the top, immediately after the header block, that names the single most important decision, the single most urgent action, and the single biggest risk raised. - Preserve the numbered section structure, the decisions box, and the action register verbatim from the source draft. Rules: do not add facts not in the draft. Do not change any owner name or due date.
Reality check: Teams Copilot recap requires a Copilot licence on the meeting organiser, and the transcript must be enabled during the recording. If the recap panel is empty, verify that Transcription was on before you left the meeting. For Word Copilot, the polish quality is only as good as the transcript quality; scrub any obvious speech-to-text errors in the transcript before running Task 4.2.

Exercise 5 · Report to DeckWord Copilot polish · PowerPoint auto-generate from file

The pipeline. A rough Q3 governance report becomes a polished Word document, then a brand-styled PowerPoint deck, then a set of speaker notes. Two Copilot moments, one clean handoff.
Task 5.1📝 Word Copilot · polish the draft

Turn a wordy draft into a board-ready governance report

Open 05 Contoso_Governance_Report_Draft.docx. Click the Copilot icon, then Rewrite with Copilot on selected sections, or apply the prompt below across the whole document.

Goal: polish this Q3 governance report so it reads like a Task Force board paper, not a working draft. Context: I am the Head of the Coordination and Supervision Task Force. This report goes to the Bureau leadership meeting on Friday. It covers procurement supervision findings across three provinces for Q3. Source: use only the content in the open Word document. Do not add new facts. Expectation: 1. Rewrite each heading in action-title style. Instead of "Findings" write something like "Three provinces show split-contract patterns above tolerance". Titles must state the conclusion, not the topic. 2. Improve sentence clarity, remove redundant phrases, and reduce jargon so a legal advisor with no procurement background can follow. 3. Insert a three-paragraph Executive Summary immediately after the report title. Paragraph one names the single most important finding. Paragraph two names the two next-most-important findings. Paragraph three states the one decision the leadership meeting is being asked to make. 4. Insert a bordered call-out box, styled to stand out, containing the single most important finding of the entire report in one sentence. 5. Keep numbered sections and any tables verbatim. Rules: no new figures. No new agency names. If a claim in the draft is not backed by data in the draft itself, flag it inline with "(source not stated in draft)".
Task 5.2🎯 PowerPoint Copilot · Create presentation from file

Auto-generate the deck from the polished Word doc, then style it

Open PowerPoint. In Copilot, choose "Create presentation from file" and point it at the polished 05 Contoso_Governance_Report_Draft.docx. When the deck is generated, run the single Edit prompt below to style and finish it.

Goal: turn this auto-generated deck into a Task Force board-ready presentation with our navy and gold brand. Context: I am presenting to the Bureau leadership meeting on Friday. Twenty minutes, twelve slides maximum. Audience includes two Deputies and the Bureau Secretariat. Source: the deck you just generated from 05 Contoso_Governance_Report_Draft.docx. Expectation: 1. Rewrite every slide title into action-title style (state the conclusion, not the topic). 2. Insert a scorecard slide right after the title slide with four big numbers: total contracts reviewed, total IDR value, count of Critical-tier contracts, count of agencies with a Critical-tier contract. Take these from the Word document body. 3. Insert a Zoom summary slide at the end that visually links to the three most important body slides. 4. Apply a colour scheme based on deep navy blue for headers and section dividers, with institutional gold as the accent for callouts and numbers. Body text in muted grey on white. Do not use bright reds or greens. 5. Add speaker notes to each body slide: three short bullets that a presenter can read in 30 seconds. 6. Add a final appendix slide titled "Sources" listing the report file name and any URLs referenced in the report. Rules: do not invent numbers not in the Word document. Do not change any agency name. Keep the slide count at or below twelve, excluding the appendix.
Reality check: PowerPoint Copilot's "Create presentation from file" feature respects the Word document's heading structure. If the source doc has weak or topic-only headings, the deck inherits them. Always run the Word polish in Task 5.1 first, save, then feed the polished file to PowerPoint. Also: Copilot in Word cannot reference Excel; the numbers must be in the Word document body already, not in a linked spreadsheet.

B0 · Governance Poster (Copilot Create · four prompts)

Open Copilot Create (formerly Designer). Pick "Design an image" or the poster template. Paste any of the four prompts below. Copilot Create generates variations; iterate until you have a poster fit to hang on the Task Force wall or drop into a Bureau leadership deck.

Poster 1🎨 Copilot Create · Q3 priorities

Q3 Anti-Corruption Priorities for the Task Force Head

Design a one-page portrait infographic poster titled "Q3 Anti-Corruption Priorities" for the Head of the Coordination and Supervision Task Force at Contoso Integrity Bureau. Use a deep navy blue as the primary colour, institutional gold as the accent, and white as the background. Muted grey for supporting text. Serious, government-grade, institutional feel; not flashy. Layout, top to bottom: - A slim navy header bar with the Bureau name on the left and "Q3 Priorities" on the right in gold. - A "big numbers" strip with four numbers side by side, each with a gold underline: Open Cases, Cases Handed to Court, State Loss Recovered in IDR, Red-Flag Agencies. Leave the numbers as placeholders like "XX" for now. - Three priority cards in a row, each with a gold icon-style circle at the top and a short caption underneath: (1) OSINT Sweep, (2) Procurement Anomaly Review, (3) Provincial Supervision Letters. - A small agency comparison table with three rows and three columns: Agency, Risk Tier, Next Step. Leave the cell values as placeholders. - A footer strip in navy with the Bureau name and a small gold seal-style circle on the right. Typography: sans-serif, generous line height, all-caps for section labels. No photographs, no logos other than the Bureau seal placeholder. No decorative emojis.
Poster 2🎨 Copilot Create · case journey

From Report to Verdict - the six-stage journey of a corruption case

Design a one-page landscape infographic poster titled "From Report to Verdict: the six-stage journey of a corruption case at Contoso Integrity Bureau". Deep navy blue primary, institutional gold accent, white background, muted grey supporting text. Institutional, quietly authoritative, not flashy. Layout: - Navy title bar at the top with the poster title in white and the Bureau name in gold on the right. - A horizontal six-stage journey strip across the middle: (1) Report received, (2) Preliminary assessment, (3) Investigation opened, (4) Suspect named, (5) Handed to court, (6) Verdict.
